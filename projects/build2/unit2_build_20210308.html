Mental Health in Tech

Survey by Open Sourcing Mental Illness, LTD circa 2014

Whether a job is fulfilling or not is not only dependent on salary or number of hours worked. Enjoying the work we do is important as well. So are fringe benefits like vacation, bonuses, and the topic of this article, health benefits.

Benefits packages generally include an array of services and features. This allows employees access to healthcare when they need it at a reasonable cost. As time goes on, there are more and more companies choosing benefits packages with mental health care benefits explicitly included.

In the following chart, we see the number of people by age who have sought assistance for a mental health issue by age. One detail that stands out is the percentage of people seeking help starts to increase at the mean age of 32. The second is that while more people did not seek help with a mental issue, the percentage of those people is very low between age 23 and 31.

Here is one issue with mental health in the workplace. This chart shows the number of people reporting a negative consequence as a direct result of their mental health issue being made public knowledge. It is evident this is less prominent in very small companies with fewer than 6 employees and companies with 100 to 1000 employees. The percentage of employees seeking help despite the possible negative consequences is highest in companies with 26 to 500 employees and those with over 1000.

This last chart shows employee overall sentiment toward the company versus if those employees feel anonymity is respected. As the sentiment score increases, so does the percentage of employees who feel there health concerncs will be kept anonymous. There is also a correlation between higher sentiment scores and employees who are willing to seek assistance with their mental health issues.

Mental health in decades past has been a taboo topic for many with stigma attached to those who admit they need assistance. Using information directly from the employees of a company, it is possible for a company to detect issues surrounding mental health within itself to better facilitate the correction of negative consequences when an issue is made public. To illustrate this, a machine learning model was able to predict the likelihood employees would seek assistance with a percieved mental health issue with 88% accuracy. This enables a company to choose benefits packages for their employees which minimize benefits that go unused, thus reducing waste of both company and employee assets. When employees are able to seek assistance and use more of what they pay for, it contributes to a better sentiment toward the company which lends itself to longevity of staff and higher profits for everyone involved.
